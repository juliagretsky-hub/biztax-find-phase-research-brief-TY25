BizTax Find Phase — TY25 Research Brief

External customers only Jan – Jun 2026 HeyMarvin: 3 confirmed BizTax projects · N=27 sessions + insight synthesis · VOC Suite: 5 searches · ~2,500 records Pulled June 9, 2026
📌
Top findings across all TY25 research — HeyMarvin (4 projects, N≈50 external customers) and Intuit VOC Suite (5 semantic searches, ~2,500 contact center records). Each finding is linked to at least one direct source. For full evidence, see the HeyMarvin and VOC tabs.
1
🔴 Critical ④ QB/TT Gap ③ Wayfinding

QuickBooks drops business owners into the wrong product — and they don't know it

Clicking "TurboTax" inside a QB business account routes customers to personal TurboTax / Schedule C, not TurboTax Business. Customers invest significant time before discovering they've been in the wrong product — or never discover it at all. One participant was unable to file for multiple years because of this routing failure.

"It seemed like there would be a reasonable expectation it would take me to a business TurboTax software...but it returned me to where I had already started."
Marnie W. · LLC/PartnershipHeyMarvin #50577
"Started automotive repair LLC, clicked 'start taxes' in QuickBooks — immediately unsure how data would transfer between the systems."
QB LLC OwnerVOC Suite Tab 4 · Rec #356
2
🔴 Critical ③ Wayfinding

Business tax is invisible to unauthenticated visitors — the entire consideration stage is cut off

The TurboTax homepage shows only "Start for Free" and "Sign In" to unauthenticated users. There is no visible path to business tax without logging in first. Customers evaluating TurboTax as an option — the core Find Phase moment — hit a dead end before they can even assess fit.

"I'm on the web page without signing in — up at the top it says 'start for free' or 'sign in.' I don't see a place where it says business taxes."
Business Tax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 4 · Rec #12
"I literally got stuck on the first page where it says 'where should we start today?' — expressing embarrassment about not being able to begin their business taxes."
Business Tax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 4 · Rec #204
3
🔴 Critical ① Brand

TurboTax isn't in the consideration set — customers don't believe it does serious business tax

Across HeyMarvin sessions and VOC contact center records, BGS customers arrive already anchored to H&R Block, local CPAs, or other tools. The TurboTax brand reads as consumer/DIY — not as a credible expert-led service for LLCs, S-Corps, or Partnerships. When customers do call in to ask, the answer often confirms their doubt.

"I'll deal with the devil I'm used to."
Tahera C. · SMLLCHeyMarvin #50577 — on staying with H&R Block
"Customer asks if TurboTax offers business tax services — told only virtual expert options, not in-person. Responds: 'never mind.'"
Business CustomerVOC Suite Tab 1 · Rec #206
4
🔴 Critical ② Expert Credibility

Customers can't see expert credentials before matching — and won't commit without them

The expert matching system shows only years of experience and Intuit course completions. No CPA/EA designation, no industry specialization, no prior business type experience. Customers — especially those with complex entities — won't book without knowing who they're getting. This is a display problem, not an expertise problem.

"I wanna see their credentials and then I will decide whether I wanna meet with that person or not."
BizTax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 3 · Rec #3
"The web interface fails to display professional qualifications, specialty areas, or experience indicators such as international tax or expat filing capabilities."
BizTax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 3 · Rec #18
5
🔴 Critical ② Expert Credibility

Customers can't tell if they're talking to AI or a human expert — and it's killing consideration

The distinction between Intuit Assist (AI) and Expert Help (human) is not legible to customers. This ambiguity doesn't just cause confusion — it devalues the expert offer before interaction starts. Multiple VOC records show customers who assumed they'd already talked to a human and received AI responses.

"I'm assuming Intuit Assist is some kind of AI agent platform and Expert Help actually would get you a human, but...unsure."
Pearson W. · Sole PropHeyMarvin #50577
"Tax Expert feature appears to use AI-generated responses rather than genuine expert knowledge."
BizTax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 3 · Rec #26
6
🔴 Critical ① Brand

Price is revealed mid-flow, not upfront — customers feel trapped or deceived

Business tax pricing by entity type is not visible at discovery. Customers invest 30–60 minutes before hitting a price that can be 3–5× higher than they expected based on the landing page offer. This pattern appeared across multiple VOC records and at least one HeyMarvin session. It reads as a commitment trap, not a complexity explanation.

"Business tax section displayed a $150 offer for expert filing — but after starting the process, a price preview showed over $775."
BizTax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 5 · Rec #211
"Let an expert file your taxes for only $150 only applies to sole proprietors...for $399 we may be able to...It's better than $1,200."
Marnie W. · LLC/PartnershipHeyMarvin #50577
7
🟠 High ③ Wayfinding

Entity type is never confirmed at entry — LLCs and S-Corps discover the wrong product mid-session

There is no front-door guard rail that confirms whether a customer's entity type is supported or routes them to the correct product. Customers who are LLCs, S-Corps, or Partnerships frequently begin a sole proprietor flow and only realize the mismatch after significant time investment — often during a live call with an agent.

"My only concern as it says it's for sole proprietorship and we're considered an LLC."
LLC CustomerHeyMarvin #50577
"Single-member LLC customer learns mid-call she should file as an individual on Schedule C — not the business product. Appointment cancelled; must start over."
Single-member LLCVOC Suite Tab 1 · Rec #139
8
🟠 High ① Brand

The landing page creates a trust deficit before any expert interaction happens

Contact center records show customers describing the TurboTax business landing page as "shady," "aggressive," and confusing before reaching a single product screen. The interrogation-first navigation model (answering questions before seeing value) blocks the exploration behavior that drives consideration.

"TurboTax's landing pages use aggressive marketing tactics that artificially inflate pricing. Customer describes the experience as 'shady' and untrustworthy."
BizTax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 2 · Rec #3
"Your landing page sucks asking me to answer questions instead of giving me the option to navigate to what I need."
BizTax CustomerVOC Suite Tab 2 · Rec #18
9
🟠 High ④ QB/TT Gap

QB data doesn't transfer cleanly — customers find accuracy gaps they can't trust

Even when customers successfully navigate to TurboTax Business via QuickBooks, the data integration fails. Chart of accounts categories don't map correctly, imports are slower than manual entry, and customers who cross-check TurboTax against other tools find discrepancies they can't explain. One participant found a ~$20k variance.

"My biggest issue was aligning the categories within the chart of accounts with the categories that TurboTax wanted them in. And that transition was never a clean conversation."
April · S-Corp, Construction, $2.8M revenueHeyMarvin #54075
"I'm just looking forward to finding another accounting software that has a good relationship with tax software. So that works well together."
Churner · IT Services, ~$10–12MHeyMarvin #54075

When the expert experience works, it creates the strongest retention and WTP signal in the research

🗺️ Navigating to the Right Product
  • Clicking TurboTax from inside a QB business account drops customers into personal TT / Schedule C — not TurboTax Business
  • No in-product confirmation of correct workflow for entity type (LLC, S-Corp, Partnership)
  • Pricing by entity type not visible at consideration — customers discover a $150–$399 gap mid-session
  • Brand inertia (H&R Block, CPAs) not overcome by the QB integration because the integration experience itself is broken
🤷 Expert Credibility at the Front Door
  • Customers cannot tell Intuit Assist (AI) from Expert Help (human) — ambiguity devalues the expert before interaction starts
  • "Tax advisor" credentials dismissed as marketing by customers with complex situations — CPA or situational match required
  • Customers who've had one bad expert interaction won't try again without upfront credibility signals
💸 Pricing and Service Level Confusion
  • S-Corp bundle pricing ambiguous: customers can't determine if the $400+ tier covers the business return or is a surcharge
  • Aggressive "chat now / live help" prompts appear before problems exist, signaling distrust of DIY ability
🔗 QuickBooks Integration Failures
  • QB Desktop chart of accounts names don't match TurboTax categories — creating tax outcome variances. One participant found ~$20k discrepancy (April, S-Corp, Construction, #54075)
  • Import feature slower and less reliable than manual entry for many users
  • Data doesn't fully transfer — customers re-enter items manually even after importing
  • High-revenue QBO customer actively seeking alternative accounting software with better tax integration — QB→TT is a churn driver for this segment
